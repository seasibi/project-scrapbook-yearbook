# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Class of 2026 graduates — a tight cohort of 26 students, plus their friends, family, and future selves looking back. They arrive from a shared link or QR code, likely on their phones, expecting something that feels like *them*: loud, personal, a little chaotic, unmistakably theirs. Secondary audience: anyone they show it to who wasn't there.

## Product Purpose
A digital yearbook and scrapbook — "The Archive" — that commemorates the graduating class of 2026. It replaces a printed yearbook with something interactive: a flippable book, searchable portraits, a photo gallery, an auth-gated dedications wall, and a global sticker layer. The site sits behind a countdown gate until July 3, 2026 (early access via password). Success looks like every graduate returning to it years later and still feeling something.

## Brand Personality
Nostalgic, handcrafted, chaotic. Warm scrapbook energy — cut-out lettering, washi tape, polaroids, notebook-grid paper — but filtered through gen-z irreverence: lowercase casual copy, pixel fonts, glitch animations, "thank u, next" as a gate title. It should feel like a shoebox of memories someone dumped on a desk and lovingly arranged, not a corporate yearbook.

## Anti-references
- Slick SaaS landing pages with gradient cards and metric dashboards
- Generic "modern minimal" portfolios (white bg, thin sans-serif, lots of whitespace)
- Over-produced school yearbook sites that feel institutional
- Pinterest-perfect scrapbooking — this should feel real, not curated
- Any UI that looks like it came from a template marketplace

## Design Principles
1. **Messy on purpose** — Imperfection is the aesthetic. Slight rotations, torn edges, overlapping elements, hand-lettered text. If it looks too clean, it's wrong.
2. **Memory over information** — Every element should trigger a feeling or memory, not just convey data. The gallery isn't a grid of photos; it's a wall of moments.
3. **One class, one artifact** — This exists for 26 specific people. Inside jokes, shared language, and personal touches belong here. It's not scalable and shouldn't be.
4. **Reveal, don't dump** — The experience unfolds as you scroll, flip, and explore. The countdown gate, the flipbook, the scroll-driven gallery — everything rewards patience and curiosity.
5. **Loud when it matters** — The ransom-note lettering, the pixel fonts, the glitch effects — these aren't decorations. They're the voice of the class. Use them at moments that deserve attention.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG AA compliance. Good contrast ratios (4.5:1 body text, 3:1 large text), keyboard navigation, screen reader support, meaningful alt text. All motion respects `prefers-reduced-motion`. Content remains visible without JS (progressive enhancement with 800ms fallback). The scrapbook aesthetic shouldn't come at the cost of readability.
